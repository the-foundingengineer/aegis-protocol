The Quincunx: The Inheritance of John Huffam

Charles Palisser

The Quincunx follows the life of a minor heir who is cheated out of his inheritance. As the hero fights to recover his estate, we’re given a tour of low-paid jobs the Victorian poor relied on to scrape together a living: searching sewers for pennies and scrubbing plates in the houses of the landed rich. The novel is a visceral reminder of how much we’ve benefited from economic growth.
